# Decision memo — AI search isn't cutting your leads, it's filtering them

**To:** Head of Growth / Demand Gen
**From:** Marketing Analytics
**Re:** Paid-search leads are down ~26%. Do we cut? **Recommendation: No — reallocate.**
**Read time:** ~2 minutes
*(Figures from a research-informed simulation; directional, not actuals.)*

---

## The situation

Total non-brand paid-search leads fell from **~662 to ~489 per week (−26%)** over
the last year, tracking the rise of AI Overviews. On the weekly leads dashboard,
paid search looks broken, and "cut it" is the obvious call.

## Why "cut it" is the wrong call

The drop is **not** a performance failure. It's a **mix shift**, concentrated in
exactly the traffic AI search is built to absorb.

- **The decline is almost entirely informational.** Informational leads −60%;
  commercial −13%; transactional −6%.
- **AI Overviews are the mechanism.** Where an AI Overview is present,
  informational lead yield is **−59%** per impression. The share of informational
  impressions behind an AI Overview rose **24% → 81%**.
- **The leads we keep are better.** Qualified-lead rate **40.6% → 52.6% (+30%)**,
  qualified leads roughly flat (**−4%**), revenue per visitor **+70%**.

Translation: AI didn't break paid search — it **filtered** it, stripping out the
cheap, low-intent clicks that rarely became real pipeline anyway.

## The money

Based on the most recent 8-week run rate:

| Option | Result (per year, at equal or zero spend) |
| --- | --- |
| **Cut the program** | Forfeit ~**13,400 qualified leads** and ~**\$10.1M revenue** — including the healthy commercial engine |
| **Reallocate** (shift 70% of informational spend to commercial-intent) | Net **+~1,700 qualified leads** and **+~\$1.3M revenue** at the **same budget** |

Cost per qualified lead tells the whole story: **informational \$958 vs
commercial-intent \$152** — roughly a 6× efficiency gap.

## What to do (next quarter)

1. **Reallocate budget** from informational queries to commercial/transactional
   intent. Stop paying for clicks AI now answers for free.
2. **Rebuild landing pages around proof** — comparison, calculators, demos, case
   studies — not generic informational content.
3. **Change the scoreboard** to **qualified-lead rate** and **revenue per
   visitor**. Retire raw leads/clicks as the headline KPI; it's the metric that
   nearly cost us the program.

**Bottom line:** Don't cut the engine because the cheap fuel ran out. Refuel it
with the traffic that still converts.
